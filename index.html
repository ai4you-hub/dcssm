  • Key Question 1: What are the components of the DeepConv family?

    Deconstructionism: This paper deconstructs the DeepConv family into a unified Meta DeepConv architecture based on DeepConv Encoder and Context-aware Block. From this fresh perspective, we introduce the effective and efficient HAR model, DeepConvSSM.


  • Key Question 2: How to better spatiotemporal modeling?

    Novel Network: To address the generalization issues of the DeepConv Encoder, we propose a simple and scalable CBRM Encoder with modern design, potentially enhancing spatiotemporal modeling. Unlike previous Context-aware blocks based on Attention, RNN, or GNN, we present the Spatio-Temporal State Space Model, ST Mamba, which firstly uses temporal and spatial scanning to jointly model activity state.


  • Key Question 3: Can CNN and SSM complement each other in HAR task?

    Complementary Advantages: This work introduces a new activity recognition architecture, CNN-SSM, demonstrating their complementary strengths. The CNN extracts better embeddings, while the SSM optimizes spatiotemporal activity features. Importantly, DeepConvSSM outperforms state-of-the-art HAR models while maintaining a low computational burden and space complexity.

Abstract

Without bells and whistles, we introduce the simple and novel structure called DeepConvSSM can serve as activity recognizer, surpassing previous state-of-the-art models (Up to 8.5%), and bringing the new network type CNN-SSM for Human Activity Recognition (HAR). Reviewing HAR models, the widely known DeepConvLSTM and its derived models (i.e. DeepConv Family) have been prominent. However, those models still have two main limits: (1) redundant feature extractor, and (2) inefficient spatio-temporal modeling. In this paper, we firstly hypothesize that the Meta DeepConv is a general HAR architecture composed of a DeepConv Encoder and a Context-aware Block. To address the lack of generalization in the DeepConv Encoder, we upgrades it to a simple yet efficient Modern DeepConv Encoder to extract pathify embeddings. From the perspective of the Context-aware Block, we propose the new Spatio-Temporal Mamba (ST Mamba), which for the first time leverages the State Space Model (SSM) to jointly model the temporal and spatial information contained in activity sensor. Interestingly, ST Mamba has shown excellent performance across various sensor modalities, revealing the potential for better modeling of HAR tasks.
